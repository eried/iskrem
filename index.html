How your machine works read me first▸

Not a churn-as-it-freezes machine: you freeze a liquid base rock solid for ~24 h, then the blade shaves it into silky ice cream. Get the base chemistry right and it's luxurious; wrong and it's an ice cube.

The food science creaminess & beating iciness▸

Four levers of creaminess

  • Fat coats ice crystals (cream, marzipan, PB, coconut, oat cream).
  • Protein traps water & air — flavour and body.
  • Sugar/allulose lower the freezing point → scoopable.
  • Stabiliser (xanthan, 0.1–0.5% of mix) holds it together.

Beat the iciness (sugar-free)

  • Allulose is the main softener — keeps it scoopable, no grit. ~2 tbsp/pot, never more or it won't freeze hard enough to churn.
  • Sukrin Melis (erythritol) crystallises gritty above ~8%; we keep it low.
  • Watery fruit/soda = ice: cook compotes down or accept sorbet/slush by design.
  • Taste the base before freezing — aim slightly too sweet.
